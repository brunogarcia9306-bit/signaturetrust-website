FEMA Flood Zones

The majority of Cape Coral properties — especially canal-front homes — are in FEMA Zone AE, the highest-risk flood designation. Flood insurance is required by most mortgage lenders in these zones and is never included in a standard homeowners policy.

New Construction

Cape Coral is one of Florida's fastest-growing cities. New construction buyers need homeowners insurance from day one, often a flood policy required by the lender, and sometimes builder's risk during the build phase. Bruno walks you through every step.

Boats & Watercraft

Common Questions

Cape Coral
Insurance FAQ

Is flood insurance required if I live on a canal in Cape Coral?+
Most Cape Coral canal properties are in FEMA Special Flood Hazard Areas (Zone AE). If you have a federally-backed mortgage on a property in these zones, flood insurance is legally required. Even without a mortgage, canal exposure means your financial risk is real — a single flood event can cause tens of thousands in damages that homeowners insurance will not cover. Flood is always a separate policy.
How much does flood insurance cost in Cape Coral?+
Flood insurance rates in Cape Coral depend on your FEMA flood zone, the elevation of your structure (measured by an elevation certificate), the property value, and your chosen deductible. NFIP rates are federally set, while private flood carriers often offer lower premiums for properties with favorable elevations. Bruno compares both NFIP and private options to find the best rate for your specific address.
Can you insure my boat or watercraft in Cape Coral?+
Yes. With direct canal access to the Gulf, Cape Coral residents are among the most active boating communities in Florida. Watercraft insurance covers physical damage to your boat, liability if you're responsible for an accident on the water, and uninsured boater coverage — both on canals and in open Gulf waters. Ask Bruno about bundling boat coverage with your home or auto for potential discounts.
I just bought a new construction in Cape Coral. What insurance do I need?+
New constructions in Cape Coral typically need homeowners insurance from the first day of occupancy — and if your lender requires it, a separate flood policy as well. If the home is still under construction, builder's risk insurance protects the structure during the build phase. Bruno guides you through the right sequence of coverage so you're never left unprotected at any stage of the process.
