Coastal Flood Risk

Properties near the Imperial River, Little Hickory Bay, and the Gulf coastline face real flood exposure — including storm surge during hurricane season. FEMA flood zones AE and VE cover much of Bonita Springs. Flood is always a separate policy and is never included in homeowners coverage.

High-Value Homes

Luxury homes in Bonita Springs require coverage that reflects true replacement cost — not market value. Custom finishes, high-end appliances, and premium construction mean standard policy limits often fall short after a major loss. Bruno works with carriers that specialize in high-value home protection.

Condo Communities

Bonita Springs has a large number of condo communities — Bonita Bay, Pelican Landing, Barefoot Beach, and more. Each has its own master policy that typically covers only the exterior and common areas. An HO-6 policy protects your interior, belongings, and personal liability — and most lenders and HOAs require it.

Common Questions

Bonita Springs
Insurance FAQ

Is flood insurance required for homes in Bonita Springs?+
Many Bonita Springs properties — especially those near the Imperial River, Little Hickory Bay, or the Gulf coast — fall within FEMA Special Flood Hazard Areas (Zone AE or VE). If you have a federally-backed mortgage on a property in these zones, flood insurance is legally required. Even outside mandatory zones, coastal proximity makes flood risk real and homeowners policies never cover flood damage. It is always a separate policy.
What is condo insurance (HO-6) and do I need it in Bonita Springs?+
HO-6 condo insurance covers what your condo association's master policy doesn't — your interior walls, flooring, fixtures, personal belongings, and personal liability. In Bonita Springs communities like Bonita Bay, Pelican Landing, and Barefoot Beach, the master policy typically covers the building exterior and common areas only. An HO-6 policy fills the gap. Many associations require owners to carry it, and lenders almost always do.
I have a seasonal or second home in Bonita Springs. Can you insure it?+
Yes. Seasonal and second homes in Bonita Springs have different risk profiles than primary residences — they may be vacant for months at a time, which some standard policies exclude or limit coverage for. Bruno places coverage through carriers that specifically accommodate seasonal and secondary homes, ensuring your Bonita Springs property is protected year-round, whether you're there or not.
How is insuring a high-value home in Bonita Springs different?+
High-value homes require coverage amounts that reflect true replacement cost — not market value. Standard policies often underinsure luxury properties, leaving owners exposed after a major loss. Bruno works with carriers that specialize in high-value homes, offering guaranteed replacement cost, extended coverage for custom finishes, and access to insurers who understand the Bonita Springs luxury market.
