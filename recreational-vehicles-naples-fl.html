Whether you're fishing off Marco Island, cruising Naples Bay at sunset, riding the coast on your motorcycle, or exploring Florida in your RV — standard auto or homeowners insurance doesn't cover any of it properly. Recreational vehicles have their own risk profile, and they need dedicated coverage built for how and where you use them in Southwest Florida.

Hurricane & Storm Exposure

Hurricane season is the biggest risk for boat owners in SWFL. Your policy needs to address haul-out requirements, named storm deductibles, and storage coverage. Many boat owners discover coverage gaps only after a storm — when it's too late. Bruno reviews the exact hurricane plan conditions in your policy before season starts.

On-Water Liability

Naples Bay, the Cape Coral canal system, and the Intracoastal Waterway are busy during season. A collision causing injury or property damage to another boater, dock, or vessel means personal liability. Without proper coverage, a single accident can result in a lawsuit with no financial protection behind you.

Uninsured Boater Risk

Unlike cars, boats are not required to carry insurance in Florida. A significant share of boaters on local waterways carry no liability coverage at all. If an uninsured boater hits you and causes injury or damage, uninsured boater coverage is the only protection you have. Without it, you pay out of pocket for someone else's mistake.

Common Questions

Boat & Recreational Vehicle
Insurance FAQ

Does my homeowners insurance cover my boat in Naples?+
Standard homeowners policies (HO-3) provide very limited or no coverage for watercraft. Most HO-3 policies exclude boats over a certain horsepower or value, and they never cover on-water liability, emergency towing, or uninsured boater claims. A dedicated boat policy provides physical damage for your vessel, motor, and trailer, plus on-water liability, emergency towing, and uninsured boater coverage — none of which a homeowners policy adequately handles.
What happens to my boat coverage during hurricane season in Southwest Florida?+
Most boat policies include hurricane coverage, but with specific conditions. Many policies require you to move the boat to a named location, haul it out of the water, or secure it according to the insurer's hurricane plan when a named storm is within a certain distance. Failing to follow these requirements can result in a denied claim. Before storm season, Bruno reviews the exact conditions in your policy so there are no surprises. Hurricane hauling expenses and storage coverage are worth discussing when placing a new boat policy in SWFL.
Is boat insurance required in Florida?+
Florida does not require boat owners to carry insurance. However, marinas often require proof of liability coverage to dock, lenders require it if you're financing the vessel, and the liability exposure of operating a boat without coverage in busy SWFL waterways is significant. In Naples Bay, the Cape Coral canal system, or the Intracoastal during season, a collision causing injury can result in a lawsuit with nothing behind you. Most Naples-area boat owners carry coverage for exactly this reason.
Can I insure my motorcycle year-round in Florida even if I only ride occasionally?+
Yes, and it usually makes sense to. Florida's year-round mild climate makes motorcycles insurable as full-time vehicles, and rates are competitive. Some policies offer reduced-premium storage options for months when the bike isn't used. If your motorcycle is financed, your lender requires comprehensive and collision year-round regardless of usage. Bruno compares options from multiple carriers based on your motorcycle type, riding frequency, and how you use it.
