Common Questions

Estero
Insurance FAQ

Do I need flood insurance in Estero, FL?+
Flood risk in Estero varies by location. Properties near Estero Bay, the Estero River, or low-lying areas within master-planned communities can fall within FEMA flood zones. If your lender requires it, flood insurance is mandatory. Even without a lender requirement, Estero's flat terrain and proximity to the Gulf mean standard homeowners insurance is not enough on its own — flood is always a separate policy.
I bought a new construction home in Estero. What insurance do I need?+
New construction homes in Estero need homeowners insurance from day one of occupancy. If your lender requires it and your property is in a flood zone, a separate flood policy is also needed. Some buyers also carry builder's risk insurance during construction to protect the structure before move-in. Bruno guides you through the right sequence so there's no gap in coverage at any stage.
Does my HOA master policy cover me in communities like Miromar Lakes or Pelican Sound?+
HOA master policies in Estero's gated communities typically cover common areas and building exteriors — not your individual home's interior, personal belongings, or personal liability. You still need your own homeowners policy to cover what the master policy leaves out. Bruno reviews your community's master policy with you to identify exactly what gaps your personal coverage needs to fill.
Can you bundle home and auto insurance in Estero?+
Yes. As an independent agent, Bruno shops multiple carriers to find the best combination of home and auto coverage for Estero residents. Bundling home and auto with the same carrier often results in meaningful discounts on both policies — Bruno compares the numbers to make sure bundling actually saves you money before recommending it.
